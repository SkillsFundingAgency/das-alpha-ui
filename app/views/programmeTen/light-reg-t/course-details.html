2. Course description

The course is divided into topics, each containing different key concepts of chemistry. Once the key features of a topic have been developed, applications are considered. The delivery methods include lectures, laboratory work and group work, with regular homework being set which plays an important part of the learning process. A range of resources is available in the Learning Resource Centre and via the VLE. Unit F321: Atoms, Bonds and Groups

Modules: 1. Atoms and reactions; 2. Electrons, bonding and structure; 3. The Periodic Table.

Assessment: 30% of the total marks for AS. 1 hour written examination taken in January.

This unit builds on ideas developed at Key Stage 4 and develops a platform of key concepts in chemistry. During this unit you will learn about: Atomic Structure and Chemical Bonding; Moles and Equations; Acid and Redox; Trends in the Periodic Table; The Chemistry of Groups 2 and 7.

Unit F322: Chains, Energy and Resources

Modules: 1. Basic concepts and hydrocarbons; 2. Alcohols, halogenoalkanes and analysis; 3. Energy; 4. Resources.

Assessment: 50% of the total marks for AS. 1¾ hour written examination taken in May/June.

This unit is an introduction to organic and physical chemistry. It also contains environmental aspects of chemistry. During this unit you will learn about Alkanes, Alkenes, Alcohols and Halogenoalkanes; IR Spectroscopy and Mass Spectrometry; Energy Changes in Chemical Reactions; Rates and Reversible Reactions; Chemistry of the Air; Green Chemistry.

Unit F323: Practical Skills in Chemistry 1

Students are assessed in qualitative, quantitative and evaluative tasks.

Assessment: 20% of the total marks for AS. Teacher assessed and externally moderated. Practical skills are developed and assessed throughout the course.

3. Entry requirements

Your qualifications should normally include a minimum of: Grade B in GCSE Chemistry (or Grade B in GCSE Science and Grade B in GCSE Additional Science) (or equivalent) and Grade B in GCSE Mathematics (or equivalent) and Grade C in GSCE English (or equivalent).

4. Equipment required

Please bring pens, pencils, paper and a scientific calculator to each class. You are expected to obtain the recommended textbook: OCR AS Chemistry A: Student Book and Exam Café CD-ROM, Dave Gent and Rob Ritchie, Heinemann, 2007, ISBN: 9780435691813.

5. Assessment method

There are two exams, usually occurring in June, that cover all of the three sciences and between them make up 75% of the total marks. There is coursework to be done during the year and this is worth 25% of the total marks. Students can sit exams at either foundation or higher tier. The maximum grade available on the foundation paper is a C, and the highest on a higher paper an A*. The higher tier papers require students to know more syllabus content as well as containing some more difficult questions. It is ultimately the student’s decision as to which paper to sit, but your tutors can give advice if required.

Automotive (craft and technician)

This information is taken from an apprenticeship framework developed by a group of employers. It sets out what the apprentice can achieve and how they can be assessed.

  • Find training providers
  • Shortlist this apprenticeship

Summary of this apprenticeship framework

Overview of role

Carrying out a range of engineering and manufacturing processes to a craft or technician level within the automotive industry.

Level

3 (equivalent to A levels at grades A to E)

Typical length

42 months

Entry requirements

Set by employer. Candidates will usually have 5 GCSEs grade C (or above) – including English, maths and a science.

What jobs apprentices can get

  • motorsport technician (mechanical)
  • motorsport technician (electrical / electronic)
  • vehicle builder (commercial and passenger carrying vehicles)
  • vehicle development technician
  • vehicle test technician

Qualifications

Apprentices can gain a range of qualifications supplied by one of the following organisations:

  • EAL
  • ETC Awards Ltd
  • IMIAL
  • Pearson

Professional registration

None specified

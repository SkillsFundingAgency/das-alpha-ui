Apprenticeship status explained

Apprenticeship training is scrutinised and approved by Government before being made available. Below is an explanation of the different stages leading up to approval apprenticeship training.

Ready for delivery (standard and assessment plan approved)

  • Means it is approved by SFA
  • Does not mean it has providers offering the course
  • Newly approved courses may not have providers ready to deliver it.
  • The problem here is the terminology is focussed around the provider (ready for delivery, instead of has providers)

Standard in development

  • Means it has not been approved yet
  • could be in early development maybe already in the approval process
  • trial blazers are responsible/point of contact at this point to find out more.
  • In development standards levels are subject to change
  • No assessment plan is complete at this point

Standard approved

  • Means the standard has been approved
  • But the assessment plan for the standard has not
  • trial blazers are responsible/point of contact at this point to find out more.

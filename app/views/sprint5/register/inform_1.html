1. Overview

Apprentices are aged 16 or over and combine working with studying for a qualification. They can be new or current employees.

If you hire an apprentice, you must:

  • employ them for at least 30 hours a week
  • pay them at least the minimum wage for apprentices
  • offer them the same conditions (eg paid holidays, sick pay and benefits) as other employees working at a similar level
  • provide your apprentice with experience and opportunities to help with future job progression
  • allow them to attend off-the-job training and assessment during their working hours (eg at a college or training organisation)

You can’t usually make an apprentice redundant simply because you can’t afford to pay them or your company runs out of work. This is because you have a contract to train them.
